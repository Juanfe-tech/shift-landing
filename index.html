01The phone breathes

The whole device floats almost imperceptibly — six-second cycle, two degrees of rotation. You don't notice it consciously. But your nervous system reads "alive object" instead of "static image."

02Atmospheric light

Three layered radial gradients on the cream — pink dawn at the top, warm shadow at the bottom, soft glow on the right. The screen is never flat. It's morning light on cotton paper.

03The seal animation

When you complete an action, the check rotates from -180° through a slight overshoot, settling into place. Feels like sealing wax. The row glows briefly. Both visual and tactile.

04The lesson card glows

Two atmospheric blooms drift inside the dark card, ten-second cycles, opposite directions. The thinking room has weather. It's not static decoration — it's a living pocket of night.

05The streak's last dot pulses

Day 12 has a soft blush halo that breathes every two seconds. It marks where you are right now. All other dots are still. Only the present moment has motion.

06The signature exhale

When you complete the evening review, a wave of warm light passes from top to bottom of the screen, accompanied by one warm bell tone. Long decay. The day seals. It happens once a day. You'll learn to expect it.

07Sound as scarcity

Three sounds total. The seal click when you complete an action. The temple bell at evening review. A soft chime at the day's first open. That's it. No notifications. No alerts. Sound becomes meaningful by being rare.

08Stagger on entry

Every element fades up sequentially — 150ms apart, expo easing. The screen composes itself. Like a painter laying down strokes. By the end you feel: this was made for you, just now.
